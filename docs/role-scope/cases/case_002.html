Case #002 — Technology License Dispute

Award (Public)

Claim dismissed; counterclaim partially upheld.

Reasons

[Arbitrators only — Full reasons]

After reviewing the license scope, disclosure history, and milestone performance, the tribunal finds (i) the claimant failed to establish a material breach of the Technology License Agreement; (ii) portions of the respondent’s counterclaim are supported by evidence of missed deliverables and underperformance against milestones M2–M3; (iii) royalty reporting discrepancies were cured within the contractual cure window and therefore do not constitute independent grounds for termination. Accordingly, the main claim is dismissed while the counterclaim is partially upheld with quantified adjustments specified in the award.

[Parties — Key grounds summary]

  • No material breach by respondent proven under Sections 4.1–4.3 of the TLA.
  • Counterclaim partially validated based on missed M2–M3 milestones.
  • Reporting issues cured within the contractual window; no termination ground.
  • Monetary adjustment and timeline remedies applied per award annex.

Full reasons and annexes are available to parties in the official portal.

[Public summary]
